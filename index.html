I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Luxembourg Centre for Logistics and Supply Chain Management, University of Luxembourg.
My research focuses on linear programming and mixed integer programming with applications in supply chain optimization, food-delivery and last-mile delivery.
Prior to joining the University of Luxembourg, I worked as Research Scientist at LINKS Foundation. At LINKS Foundation I worked on the development of a logistics optimization model that was part of the INTERREG Italy-Switzerland project "Typicity Innovation and Competitiveness n Alpine Diary Products" (TYPICALP). In 2021, I received a Ph.D. in Operations Research from Roma Tre University, where I worked under the supervision of Prof. Gaia Nicosia. In 2019 and 2020 I was visiting scholar at Karl-Franzens-Universität (University of Graz, Austria) where I worked on a mixed integer programming model for the scheduling and deployment of emergency and non-emergency vehicles. This project was carried out in collaboration with the Austrian Red Cross, under the guidance of Prof. Ulrich Pferschy. As a follow up project, I submitted a proposal for the 2022 Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions (MSCA) call. The project was positively evaluated, although it was not funded given the budgetary resources available for the call.
The focus of my PhD was on a Meal-Delivery application and it was developed in collaboration with an Italian Food Delivery company. This project was carried out in collaboration with Prof. G. Oriolo (University of Roma "Tor Vergata"), Prof. Veronica Piccialli ("La Sapienza" University) and Prof. Paolo Ventura (IASI-CNR and Siemens Mobility).
I hold a B.Sc. degree and an M.Sc. degree in Management Engineering from Univeristy of Roma "Tor Vergata".

Key areas of interest. last-mile delivery; supply chain optimization; scheduling, fairness, routing, mixed-integer programming.
